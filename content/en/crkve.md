# Churches

---

## Church of Saint Stephen (Grohote)
![Parish Church of Saint Stephen](https://smn.hr/images/galerije/zupe/grohote/grohote_solta_sv_stjepan.jpg)

Construction of the Parish Church of Saint Stephen began in 1913 on the site of the old, demolished church. The First World War slowed its building. Regular liturgical services had certainly begun by 1927, even though the church was not yet fully completed. In 1938 the main altar, a work by sculptor Ante Frank of Split, was installed. The church is an imposing and magnificent structure with three naves. The façade features a bell tower with a prominent portal and an eight-pointed stone rose window, with a public clock below the bells. On the belfry floor there are two arched openings on each of the four sides. The bell tower is crowned with a slender pyramid and cross. Along the walls of the naves are five arched windows on each side. Sacristies are placed on either side of the large semicircular apse. A collection of ecclesiastical artworks is arranged in the northern sacristy. The church has been repaired several times. During the pastorate of Fr. Špiro Vuković (1975–1982) stained-glass windows were installed, and a mosaic depicting Croatian saints and candidates for beatification — a work by Fr. Ante Škobaljis — was placed on the main altar. Other mosaics in the church are also by the same artist. In the northern nave stands a Baroque altar of Our Lady of Mount Carmel from the old church. The other two side altars are more recent works of lesser artistic value. A people's altar has been placed in front of the main altar. The church holds several objects of artistic value protected as movable cultural monuments. Among these, a painting of the Assumption of Our Lady — a work of the Flemish painter Pieter de Coster from the 17th century — is particularly notable, as is a gilded reliquary from the 15th century. The church contains statues of St. Anthony, St. Joseph, St. Stephen, Our Lady of Mount Carmel, Our Lady of Fátima, St. Theresa and St. Michael. The most recent restoration of the church was carried out during the pastorate of Fr. Ivan Barišić (1982–1990).

![Early Christian Basilica](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/01/Grohote%2C_z%C3%A1klady_b%C3%BDval%C3%A9ho_kostela_02.jpg)

The present parish church was built on the site of the 14th-century church that preceded it. That earlier church was enlarged twice before finally being demolished in 1913 to make way for the current magnificent structure. Along the northern wall of today's parish church stand the conserved foundations of an early Christian basilica from the late 5th or early 6th century. As mentioned above, this early Christian basilica was burned by raiders from Omiš in 1241. Expert examination of the site was carried out in 1978. The site was protected and entered in the Register of Immovable Cultural Monuments by the Conservation Institute for Dalmatia in Split in 1962.

## Church of Saint Michael (Srednje Selo / Grohote)
![Church of Saint Michael](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e1/Crkva_sv._Mihovila_u_Grohotama.jpg)

The Church of Saint Michael stands at the edge of the field between Grohote and Srednje Selo. It was built in the 14th century and decorated with frescoes whose remains are still visible today. The church has a semicircular apse, a bell cote at the top of the façade, and is covered with stone slabs. The floor is also paved with stone. In the 19th century two arched windows were opened. The interior is vaulted with a broken Gothic vault. The altar is raised on one step; its base is a stone monolith cut at an angle on the left side, suggesting it may be a fragment of a sarcophagus serving as the stone mensa. The church is protected as a cultural monument. A cemetery for plague victims from across the island was located beside it. The church stands on an ancient sacred site and is the oldest surviving church on the island, first mentioned in 1398. It is 7.15 metres long and 4.85 metres wide.

## Church of Saint Teresa of Ávila (Rogač)
![Church of Saint Teresa of Ávila](https://smn.hr/images/galerije/zupe/grohote/grohote_solta_rogac_sv_terezija_avilska.jpg)

The Church of Saint Teresa of Ávila in Rogač was built in the 17th century. On the altar is a painting depicting Saint Teresa and the donor Nikola Gvozdenović (Ferro), who died in 1672; the painting was commissioned in 1749. The church is 17 metres long and 8 metres wide. It contains votive paintings of ships donated by sailors and sea captains, a painting of Our Lady of Sorrows, and the banner of Saint Nicholas bearing the inscription: "Croatian Society of St. Nicholas the Traveller, Chicago, 1913." The church also holds statues of Saint Teresa and Saint Nicholas. During the pastorate of Fr. Špiro Vuković a bell cote for two bells was added to the church, together with a people's altar inside.

## Chapel of Our Lady of Lourdes (Donje Selo)
![Chapel of Our Lady of Lourdes](https://smn.hr/images/galerije/zupe/grohote/grohote_solta_gospa_lurdska.jpg)

The Chapel of Our Lady of Lourdes was built of dressed stone in 1910. The entrance has a semicircular lintel and the windows are also arched. The façade features a round stone window, and at its top a bell cote for one bell, added in 1979. The chapel is covered with a flat tile roof. Inside are a statue of Our Lady of Lourdes and the Stations of the Cross.

## Chapel of Our Lady of the Gate of Dawn (Nečujam)
![Chapel of Our Lady of the Gate of Dawn](https://smn.hr/images/galerije/zupe/grohote/grohote_solta_necujam_gospa_ostrovska.jpg)

The Chapel of Our Lady of the Gate of Dawn in Nečujam was built of finely dressed stone in 1938 and was erected by the Bartelmus family from Poland. The altar is made of oak. The image of Our Lady is framed in gilded brass. The Polish inscription reads: "We fly to your protection — Ema and Jozef Bartelmus." The chapel measures 9 × 4 metres and is covered with a flat tile roof. At the top of the façade is a bell cote for one bell.

It is worth noting that the remains of the apse of the old Church of Saint Peter are still visible in Nečujam today. That church was built in the 14th century. The remains were protected as a cultural monument in 1963. Next to the church stand the ruins of the building where the Croatian Renaissance writer Marko Marulić stayed, reflected and wrote.
